# Intellectual Property and Confidentiality Policy - Acme Corporation Legal

## Purpose

This policy explains how Acme protects its intellectual property (IP) and confidential information, and how employees should handle the IP and confidential information of others.

## Intellectual Property Ownership

### Employee-Created IP

Unless agreed otherwise in writing, Acme owns:

- Software, code, and algorithms developed during employment
- Product designs, documentation, and specifications
- Marketing materials and creative content created for Acme
- Inventions and process improvements related to Acme's business

Employees are required to sign invention assignment and confidentiality agreements as a condition of employment.

### Use of Open Source Software

- Use only approved open source licenses in company projects.
- Comply with license obligations (e.g., attribution, source code disclosure where applicable).
- Consult Legal and Engineering leadership before incorporating copyleft licenses (e.g., GPL) into proprietary products.

## Confidential Information

Confidential information includes, but is not limited to:

- Product roadmaps and unreleased features
- Source code and technical designs
- Customer and prospect lists
- Pricing, margins, and financial results (prior to public release)
- Internal policies, strategies, and forecasts
- Security configurations and incident details

### Handling Confidential Information

Employees must:

- Access confidential data only when needed for their role.
- Use secure channels (VPN, encrypted storage) for sensitive documents.
- Avoid discussing confidential topics in public or unsecure environments.
- Mark documents as **"Confidential"** where appropriate.

## Third-Party Confidential Information

Acme also receives confidential information from customers, vendors, and partners.

- Treat third-party confidential information with the same care as Acme's.
- Comply with contractual confidentiality obligations.
- Do not copy or use third-party materials outside the agreed scope.

## Public Communications

Only authorized spokespeople may:

- Speak to the press or analysts about company matters.
- Make public statements about legal issues, disputes, or investigations.

Employees should not:

- Disclose non-public information on social media.
- Share screenshots of internal tools or metrics publicly.

## Leaving the Company

Upon leaving Acme, employees must:

- Return all company devices and materials.
- Not retain copies of code, documents, or other company IP.
- Continue to honor confidentiality obligations after employment ends.

## Questions and Reporting

- For IP questions: ip-questions@acme-corp.com
- For confidentiality concerns: legal@acme-corp.com

Violations of this policy may lead to disciplinary action and, in serious cases, legal remedies.
