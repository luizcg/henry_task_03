# Employee Inventions and IP Assignment FAQ

This FAQ explains how Acme Corporation handles intellectual property (IP) created by employees and contractors.

## 1. Who owns what I create at work?

In general, Acme owns intellectual property that you create:

- In the course of your employment.
- Using company time, resources, or confidential information.

This includes software code, designs, documentation, and other work product related to your job.

## 2. What about side projects?

Side projects are typically owned by the employee if they are:

- Developed entirely on personal time.
- Created without using company resources or confidential information.
- Not related to Acme's actual or reasonably anticipated business.

If there is potential overlap, employees should consult Legal or HR.

## 3. Do I need to disclose inventions?

Yes. Many employment agreements require employees to disclose inventions that may relate to the company's business, so that ownership can be assessed.

Disclosure helps avoid future disputes and ensures that both employee and company understand their rights.

## 4. What is an IP assignment agreement?

An **IP assignment agreement** is a contract where you agree to assign certain rights in your work product and inventions to Acme.

- It clarifies ownership of work created in your role.
- It may include carve-outs for prior or unrelated inventions.

## 5. Can I reuse code from previous jobs?

Employees must not bring confidential or proprietary code from previous employers into Acme's codebase.

- Open source components may be used if they comply with Acme's open source policy.
- Personal libraries should be reviewed if there is any risk of overlap with prior employers' IP.

## 6. What about open source contributions?

Open source contributions related to Acme's business may require review and approval.

- Employees should follow the internal open source contribution process.
- Licensing terms must be compatible with Acme's products and services.

## 7. Leaving the Company

When leaving Acme:

- You must return company devices and materials.
- You may not take proprietary code, data, or confidential information.
- You remain bound by confidentiality and IP assignment obligations.

## 8. Getting Help

Questions about IP ownership or assignment should be directed to the Legal team or HR. They can:

- Review specific situations.
- Clarify the effect of your employment agreement.

Understanding IP rules helps protect both employees and Acme, and supports fair innovation practices.
