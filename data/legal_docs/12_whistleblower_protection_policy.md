# Whistleblower Protection Policy

## Purpose

Acme Corporation is committed to maintaining the highest standards of ethical conduct and legal compliance. This policy establishes protections for employees who report suspected violations of law, regulation, or company policy in good faith.

## Scope

This policy applies to:
- All employees, officers, and directors
- Contractors and temporary workers
- Former employees (for matters during employment)
- Anyone who reports concerns through approved channels

## Protected Disclosures

### What Can Be Reported

Employees are encouraged to report:
- Fraud, theft, or financial irregularities
- Violations of laws or regulations
- Safety hazards or environmental violations
- Discrimination or harassment
- Conflicts of interest
- Violations of company policies
- Retaliation against whistleblowers
- Any other ethical concerns

### Good Faith Requirement

Reports must be made in good faith:
- Based on reasonable belief that a violation occurred
- Not made with malicious intent
- Not knowingly false or misleading

Reports made in good faith are protected even if the investigation does not confirm the concern.

## Reporting Channels

### Internal Channels

1. **Direct Supervisor**
   - Appropriate for most concerns
   - Fastest resolution for operational issues

2. **Human Resources**
   - hr@acmecorp.com
   - For workplace conduct issues

3. **Legal/Compliance Department**
   - compliance@acmecorp.com
   - For legal and regulatory concerns

4. **Internal Audit**
   - audit@acmecorp.com
   - For financial and accounting concerns

5. **Ethics Hotline (Anonymous)**
   - Phone: 1-800-XXX-XXXX
   - Web: ethics.acmecorp.com
   - Available 24/7
   - Operated by independent third party
   - Anonymous reporting available

### External Channels

Employees may also report to:
- Government regulatory agencies
- Law enforcement
- Other legally authorized bodies

Acme does not discourage external reporting and will not retaliate against employees who make lawful reports to authorities.

## Confidentiality

### Identity Protection

- Reporter identity kept confidential to extent possible
- Shared only on need-to-know basis for investigation
- Anonymous reporting available through Ethics Hotline
- Confidentiality maintained even if report not substantiated

### Investigation Confidentiality

- Investigations conducted discreetly
- Information shared only as necessary
- Witnesses expected to maintain confidentiality
- Results communicated on need-to-know basis

## Non-Retaliation Policy

### Prohibited Retaliation

Retaliation against whistleblowers is strictly prohibited, including:
- Termination or demotion
- Salary reduction or denial of promotion
- Reassignment to less desirable position
- Harassment or intimidation
- Negative performance reviews as retaliation
- Exclusion from meetings or projects
- Any other adverse employment action

### Who Is Protected

Protection extends to:
- The person making the report
- Anyone who assists in an investigation
- Anyone who refuses to participate in wrongdoing
- Family members of whistleblowers (from third-party retaliation)

### Reporting Retaliation

If you experience retaliation:
1. Report immediately through any channel listed above
2. Document the retaliatory actions
3. Preserve any evidence
4. Contact Legal or HR directly if urgent

Retaliation claims are investigated with the same rigor as the original report.

## Investigation Process

### Receipt of Report

1. Report acknowledged within 48 hours (unless anonymous)
2. Assigned to appropriate investigator
3. Initial assessment of severity and scope

### Investigation

1. Conducted promptly and thoroughly
2. Interviews with relevant parties
3. Document and evidence review
4. Fair process for all involved

### Resolution

1. Findings documented
2. Appropriate corrective action taken
3. Reporter notified of outcome (if identifiable)
4. Follow-up to prevent recurrence

### Timeframe

- Simple matters: 2-4 weeks
- Complex investigations: 1-3 months
- Reporter updated on significant delays

## Protections Under Law

### Federal Protections

Various federal laws protect whistleblowers:
- Sarbanes-Oxley Act (financial fraud)
- Dodd-Frank Act (securities violations)
- False Claims Act (government contract fraud)
- OSHA regulations (safety concerns)

### State Protections

Additional protections may apply under state law. Consult Legal for specific questions.

### SEC Whistleblower Program

For securities violations, employees may be eligible for:
- Financial awards (10-30% of sanctions over $1 million)
- Enhanced confidentiality protections
- Direct reporting to SEC permitted

## Responsibilities

### All Employees

- Report suspected violations promptly
- Cooperate in investigations
- Maintain confidentiality
- Do not retaliate against reporters

### Managers

- Create an environment where reporting is safe
- Take all reports seriously
- Escalate appropriately
- Monitor for and prevent retaliation
- Document and report concerns to appropriate channels

### Senior Leadership

- Set tone at the top for ethical culture
- Ensure adequate resources for investigations
- Hold violators accountable regardless of position
- Review and act on investigation findings

## False Reports

### Intentionally False Reports

Making knowingly false accusations:
- Is not protected under this policy
- May result in disciplinary action
- May result in legal consequences

### Distinguishing False from Unsubstantiated

- Unsubstantiated: Not enough evidence to confirm (protected)
- False: Reporter knew claims were untrue (not protected)

Good faith reports that cannot be proven are still protected.
