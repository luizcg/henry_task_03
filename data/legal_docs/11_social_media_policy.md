# Social Media Policy

## Overview

This policy governs the use of social media by Acme Corporation employees, whether for official company purposes or personal use that may affect the company. All employees must understand their responsibilities when engaging on social media platforms.

## Scope

This policy applies to:
- All employees, contractors, and interns
- All social media platforms (LinkedIn, Twitter/X, Facebook, Instagram, TikTok, YouTube, blogs, forums, etc.)
- Both company-sponsored and personal social media activity

## Official Company Social Media

### Authorized Spokespersons
Only designated employees may post on behalf of Acme Corporation:
- Corporate Communications team
- Marketing department (approved campaigns only)
- Executives (with Communications review)
- Designated subject matter experts (with training)

### Approval Process
All official company posts require:
1. Draft review by Communications team
2. Legal review for claims, comparisons, or sensitive topics
3. Final approval from Communications Director
4. Scheduled posting through official tools

### Brand Guidelines
Official posts must:
- Use approved company logos and imagery
- Follow brand voice guidelines
- Include required disclaimers where applicable
- Tag official company accounts only

## Personal Social Media

### General Guidelines

Employees may use personal social media, but must:
- Not claim to speak for Acme Corporation
- Not share confidential company information
- Not disparage the company, colleagues, customers, or partners
- Not use company logos without permission

### Disclosure Requirements

When discussing topics related to your work:
- Disclose your employment: "I work for Acme Corporation"
- Add a disclaimer: "Views are my own and not those of my employer"
- Do not imply company endorsement of your opinions

### Prohibited Content

Never post about:
- Confidential business information
- Unreleased products or features
- Financial information or projections
- Customer names or details without consent
- Trade secrets or proprietary methods
- Legal matters or pending litigation
- Colleague personal information

## Industry-Specific Considerations

### Customer References
- Never name customers without explicit written consent
- Case studies require Legal and customer approval
- Screenshots of customer data are prohibited

### Competitive Information
- Do not compare our products to competitors without Legal review
- Avoid disparaging competitors
- Do not share competitive intelligence gathered from social media

### Regulated Communications
If you work in a regulated area (finance, healthcare, etc.):
- Additional restrictions may apply
- Consult Legal before any industry-related posts
- Certain claims may require disclaimers

## Responding to Inquiries

### Media and Press
If contacted by media through social media:
1. Do not respond directly
2. Forward the inquiry to media@acmecorp.com
3. Communications will handle the response

### Customer Complaints
If you see customer complaints on social media:
1. Do not respond publicly
2. Forward to social@acmecorp.com
3. Customer service will address through proper channels

### Negative Comments About the Company
If you see negative posts about Acme:
1. Do not engage in arguments
2. Report concerning content to Communications
3. Let official channels handle responses

## Monitoring and Enforcement

### Company Monitoring
Acme Corporation may monitor:
- Official company social media accounts
- Publicly available posts mentioning the company
- Social media activity on company devices

We do not monitor private personal accounts, but public posts may be observed.

### Violations
Violations of this policy may result in:
- Verbal or written warning
- Removal of social media posting privileges
- Disciplinary action up to termination
- Legal action for serious breaches

### Reporting Concerns
Report social media policy concerns to:
- Your manager
- Human Resources
- Legal department
- Anonymous ethics hotline

## Best Practices

### Think Before You Post
- Would you be comfortable if your post appeared in the news?
- Could this harm the company, customers, or colleagues?
- Is this something you'd say in a business meeting?

### Protect Your Privacy
- Review your privacy settings regularly
- Be cautious about sharing location data
- Remember that "private" posts can become public

### Represent Yourself Well
- Your online presence reflects on you professionally
- Colleagues, customers, and recruiters may see your posts
- Maintain professionalism even in personal accounts

## Social Media During Work Hours

- Incidental personal use is permitted during breaks
- Excessive use during work hours is not permitted
- Official company social media work should be during business hours

## Crisis Situations

During company crises or emergencies:
1. Do not post about the situation
2. Wait for official Communications guidance
3. Share only approved company statements if asked
4. Direct all inquiries to official channels
