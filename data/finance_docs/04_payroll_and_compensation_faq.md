# Payroll and Compensation FAQ - Acme Corporation Finance

## Overview

This document answers common questions about payroll timing, bonuses, salary changes, and tax documents for Acme employees.

## Payroll Schedule

### Pay Frequency

- US employees: **bi-weekly** (every other Friday)
- Some international locations: **monthly** (last business day of the month)

### Cut-Off Dates

- Timesheets and overtime must be submitted and approved by **Monday at 5:00 PM local time** for inclusion in that week’s payroll.
- Changes to direct deposit or tax elections may take **one payroll cycle** to take effect.

## Viewing Pay Stubs

- Access pay statements at **hr.acme-corp.com** → Payroll → Pay Statements.
- Digital pay stubs include: gross pay, deductions, taxes, and net pay.

## Direct Deposit

### Setting Up Direct Deposit

1. Log into the HR Portal.
2. Navigate to **Payroll → Direct Deposit**.
3. Enter your bank routing and account numbers.
4. Select how to allocate (100% to one account, or split across multiple).

### Changes and Timing

- Changes submitted **before the payroll cut-off** will usually apply to the next paycheck.
- If direct deposit fails (e.g., invalid account), a paper check will be issued while you correct the information.

## Overtime and Premium Pay

- Non-exempt employees are eligible for overtime according to local law.
- Overtime must be **pre-approved** by your manager.
- Overtime is calculated based on hours worked over the standard threshold (e.g., 40 hours per week in the US).

## Bonuses and Incentive Pay

### Types of Bonuses

- **Annual performance bonuses** – Linked to company and individual performance.
- **Sales commissions** – Based on sales targets and commission plans.
- **Spot bonuses** – One-time recognition for exceptional contributions.

### Payment Timing

- Annual bonuses: typically paid in **Q1** of the following year after performance reviews.
- Commissions: paid on a **monthly or quarterly** schedule, depending on plan.

### Taxes on Bonuses

- Bonuses are typically taxed at supplemental income rates as required by law.
- Actual tax impact depends on your personal tax situation.

## Salary Changes

### Merit Increases

- Reviewed annually during the performance cycle.
- Effective dates and percentages are communicated by managers.

### Promotions

- Promotions may include salary adjustments.
- Effective date is typically the first day of the month following approval.

### Market Adjustments

- Occasionally, salaries may be adjusted to remain competitive with market benchmarks.

## Deductions

Common paycheck deductions include:

- Federal, state, and local taxes (where applicable)
- Social security and Medicare (US)
- Employee benefit contributions (health, dental, vision, etc.)
- Retirement plan contributions (401(k) or equivalent)
- Other voluntary deductions (e.g., ESPP, charitable contributions)

If you see a deduction you do not recognize, contact Payroll.

## Tax Forms

### Annual Tax Documents

- US: **W-2** forms are issued by **January 31** for the prior tax year.
- Contractors may receive **1099** forms as required.
- Other countries receive equivalent local forms (e.g., T4 in Canada).

### Accessing Tax Forms

- Available electronically via the HR/Payroll Portal.
- Paper copies may be mailed to your address on file if local law requires.

## Termination and Final Pay

### Voluntary Resignation

- Final paycheck timing depends on local law and company policy.
- Accrued, unused PTO may be paid out up to the policy limit (see HR PTO policy).

### Involuntary Termination

- Final pay timing is governed by local law (often faster than normal payroll cycle).
- Company property must be returned as part of the offboarding checklist.

## Common Questions

**Q: My paycheck amount is different than last period. Why?**
A: Changes may be due to overtime, bonus/commission, benefit changes, tax changes, or unpaid leave.

**Q: Who do I contact about a payroll error?**
A: Email **payroll@acme-corp.com** with your name, employee ID, pay date, and a description of the issue.

**Q: Can Payroll provide personal tax advice?**
A: No. Payroll can explain how amounts were calculated, but cannot provide individual tax advice.
