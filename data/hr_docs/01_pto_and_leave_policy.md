# PTO and Leave Policy - Acme Corporation

## Overview

Acme Corporation provides comprehensive paid time off (PTO) benefits to support employee well-being and work-life balance. This policy applies to all full-time employees after completing their probationary period.

## PTO Accrual Rates

### Full-Time Employees

PTO accrues based on years of service with the company:

- **0-2 years of service**: 15 days (120 hours) per year, accruing at 4.62 hours per pay period
- **3-5 years of service**: 20 days (160 hours) per year, accruing at 6.15 hours per pay period
- **6-10 years of service**: 25 days (200 hours) per year, accruing at 7.69 hours per pay period
- **10+ years of service**: 30 days (240 hours) per year, accruing at 9.23 hours per pay period

### Part-Time Employees

Part-time employees working at least 20 hours per week accrue PTO on a prorated basis calculated as: (weekly hours / 40) Ã— full-time accrual rate.

## PTO Request Process

### Submitting Requests

All PTO requests must be submitted through the HR Portal (hr.acme-corp.com) at least:
- **1-3 days off**: 2 weeks advance notice
- **4-5 days off**: 3 weeks advance notice
- **1-2 weeks off**: 1 month advance notice
- **More than 2 weeks**: 2 months advance notice

### Approval Workflow

1. Employee submits request in HR Portal
2. Direct manager receives notification and reviews request
3. Manager approves or denies within 5 business days
4. Employee receives email confirmation of decision
5. Approved PTO automatically updates in payroll system

### Emergency PTO

For unexpected emergencies (illness, family emergency, etc.), employees should:
1. Notify their manager as soon as possible via phone or text
2. Submit formal PTO request in HR Portal within 24 hours of return
3. Provide documentation if absence exceeds 3 consecutive days

## PTO Carryover and Payout

### Annual Carryover

Employees may carry over unused PTO to the following year with these limits:
- Maximum carryover: 40 hours (5 days)
- Carried-over PTO must be used by March 31st of the new year
- PTO exceeding carryover limits will be forfeited on December 31st

### PTO Payout at Termination

Upon voluntary resignation or termination:
- Employees receive payout for accrued, unused PTO up to 80 hours maximum
- Payout calculated at current hourly rate (salary / 2080 for exempt employees)
- Payout included in final paycheck

## Sick Leave

### Sick Leave Allocation

In addition to PTO, employees receive dedicated sick leave:
- **All employees**: 10 sick days (80 hours) per year
- Sick leave does not accrue; full allocation available January 1st
- Unused sick leave does not carry over or pay out

### Acceptable Use of Sick Leave

Sick leave may be used for:
- Personal illness or injury
- Medical appointments (doctor, dentist, specialist)
- Care for immediate family member (spouse, child, parent)
- Mental health days (maximum 2 per year without documentation)

### Documentation Requirements

Medical documentation required when:
- Absence exceeds 3 consecutive days
- Pattern of Monday/Friday absences detected
- Requested by HR for FMLA qualification

## Family and Medical Leave (FMLA)

### Eligibility

Employees are eligible for FMLA if they have:
- Worked for Acme Corporation for at least 12 months
- Worked at least 1,250 hours in the 12 months before leave
- Work at a location with 50+ employees within 75 miles

### FMLA Entitlement

Eligible employees may take up to 12 weeks of unpaid, job-protected leave per year for:
- Birth and care of newborn child
- Placement of child for adoption or foster care
- Care for immediate family member with serious health condition
- Medical leave when unable to work due to serious health condition

### FMLA Process

1. Notify HR at least 30 days in advance when leave is foreseeable
2. Complete FMLA Request Form (available on HR Portal)
3. Provide medical certification within 15 calendar days
4. HR will designate leave as FMLA-qualifying within 5 business days
5. Maintain contact with HR during leave (weekly check-ins required)

## Parental Leave

### Birth Parents

Birth parents receive:
- 12 weeks paid parental leave at 100% salary
- Additional 4 weeks paid leave at 60% salary (optional)
- May be combined with FMLA for extended unpaid leave

### Non-Birth Parents

Non-birth parents (including adoptive and foster parents) receive:
- 6 weeks paid parental leave at 100% salary
- Additional 2 weeks paid leave at 60% salary (optional)

### Parental Leave Process

1. Notify HR and manager at least 60 days before expected leave start
2. Submit Parental Leave Request Form
3. Provide documentation (due date confirmation, adoption papers, etc.)
4. Work with manager to create coverage plan
5. Complete return-to-work meeting with HR before resuming duties

## Bereavement Leave

### Immediate Family

For death of immediate family member (spouse, child, parent, sibling):
- 5 days paid bereavement leave
- Additional unpaid leave may be approved by manager

### Extended Family

For death of extended family member (grandparent, in-law, aunt, uncle, cousin):
- 3 days paid bereavement leave

### Documentation

Employees may be asked to provide:
- Obituary or death certificate
- Funeral program
- Documentation of relationship

## Jury Duty and Voting Leave

### Jury Duty

Employees summoned for jury duty receive:
- Full pay for up to 10 days of jury service
- Additional unpaid leave for extended jury service
- Must provide jury summons to HR
- Must return to work on days dismissed early

### Voting Leave

Employees may take up to 2 hours paid leave to vote if:
- Polls are not open 2 hours before or after scheduled shift
- Must request at least 2 days in advance
- Must provide voter registration if requested

## Military Leave

### Short-Term Military Leave

For annual training or weekend drills:
- Up to 15 days paid military leave per year
- Must provide military orders to HR

### Extended Military Leave

For active duty deployment:
- Unpaid leave for duration of service
- Job protection under USERRA
- Benefits continuation options available
- Reemployment rights upon return

## Holiday Schedule

### Company Holidays

Acme Corporation observes the following paid holidays:
1. New Year's Day (January 1)
2. Martin Luther King Jr. Day (Third Monday in January)
3. Presidents' Day (Third Monday in February)
4. Memorial Day (Last Monday in May)
5. Independence Day (July 4)
6. Labor Day (First Monday in September)
7. Thanksgiving Day (Fourth Thursday in November)
8. Day After Thanksgiving (Fourth Friday in November)
9. Christmas Eve (December 24)
10. Christmas Day (December 25)

### Floating Holidays

In addition to company holidays, employees receive:
- 2 floating holidays per year
- May be used for religious observances, birthdays, or personal occasions
- Must be scheduled at least 1 week in advance
- Do not carry over to next year

## Leave of Absence (Unpaid)

### Personal Leave of Absence

Employees may request unpaid personal leave for:
- Extended travel
- Educational pursuits
- Personal matters not covered by other leave types

### Requirements

- Minimum 1 year of employment
- Maximum 30 days per calendar year
- Manager and HR approval required
- Benefits may be affected (see HR for details)
