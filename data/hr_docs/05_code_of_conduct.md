# Code of Conduct - Acme Corporation

## Purpose

The Acme Corporation Code of Conduct sets expectations for how we work together, how we treat our customers and partners, and how we represent the company. It applies to all employees, contractors, and temporary staff across all regions.

## Core Principles

1. **Integrity** – We act honestly and ethically in all situations.
2. **Respect** – We treat everyone with dignity and professionalism.
3. **Compliance** – We follow all applicable laws, regulations, and company policies.
4. **Accountability** – We take responsibility for our actions and their impact.
5. **Customer Focus** – We put customers at the center of our decisions.

## Professional Behavior

### Respectful Workplace

Employees must:
- Treat colleagues, customers, and partners fairly and respectfully.
- Avoid harassment, bullying, or discriminatory behavior.
- Use inclusive language and behavior.

Prohibited behaviors include, but are not limited to:
- Offensive jokes or slurs
- Unwelcome physical contact
- Intimidation or threats
- Displaying offensive images or materials

### Diversity and Inclusion

Acme is committed to an inclusive environment where differences are valued. We do not tolerate discrimination based on:
- Race or ethnicity
- National origin
- Gender or gender identity
- Sexual orientation
- Religion
- Age
- Disability
- Marital or veteran status

### Harassment and Discrimination

Harassment or discrimination of any kind is strictly prohibited. Employees who experience or witness such behavior should:
1. If safe, tell the person that the behavior is unwelcome.
2. Report the incident to their manager, HR, or the Ethics Hotline.
3. Cooperate with any investigation.

Retaliation against anyone who makes a good-faith report is prohibited.

## Conflicts of Interest

### Identifying Conflicts

A conflict of interest occurs when personal interests could improperly influence professional decisions. Examples include:
- Doing business with a company owned by a family member
- Having a second job that competes with Acme
- Accepting expensive gifts from vendors

### Disclosure

Employees must disclose potential conflicts to HR or the Legal team. Acme will review and determine whether mitigation or recusal is required.

## Gifts and Entertainment

### General Rules

Gifts and entertainment must never:
- Influence, or appear to influence, a business decision
- Create a sense of obligation

### Acceptable Gifts

As a guideline, employees may accept:
- Promotional items of nominal value (mugs, pens, t-shirts)
- Occasional meals under $75 per person
- Reasonable entertainment with a legitimate business purpose

### Prohibited Gifts

Employees may not accept:
- Cash or cash equivalents (gift cards, vouchers)
- Excessive or lavish entertainment
- Travel or accommodation paid by vendors without Legal approval

When in doubt, consult your manager or Legal.

## Confidential Information

### Protecting Company Information

Employees must protect confidential and proprietary information, including:
- Customer data
- Financial results before public release
- Product roadmaps and source code
- Pricing strategies

Do not share confidential information with:
- Unauthorised colleagues
- Friends or family members
- Social media or public forums

### Handling Customer Data

Customer data must be:
- Collected and used only for legitimate business purposes
- Stored securely with appropriate access controls
- Shared only with authorised parties and in compliance with data protection laws

If you suspect a data incident or breach, report immediately to Security or IT.

## Use of Company Assets

### Physical Assets

Use company equipment (laptops, phones, access badges) responsibly:
- Lock your computer when away from your desk
- Do not share your badge or grant tailgaters access
- Report lost or stolen devices immediately

### Digital Assets

Use company networks and systems responsibly:
- Follow IT security policies
- Do not install unapproved software
- Do not bypass security controls or monitoring

Limited personal use of company devices is acceptable if it does not:
- Interfere with work
- Violate any law or policy
- Expose the company to risk

## Social Media and Public Communications

### Representing Acme

Only authorised spokespeople may speak on behalf of Acme publicly. If you are not an authorised spokesperson, do not:
- Comment on company strategy or financial performance
- Share internal communications or confidential information
- Represent personal opinions as company positions

### Personal Social Media

When posting on personal accounts:
- Be respectful and professional
- Do not disclose confidential or proprietary information
- Avoid negative comments about colleagues, customers, or the company

If you identify yourself as an Acme employee online, clarify that views are your own.

## Compliance with Laws and Regulations

Acme expects employees to follow all applicable laws, including:
- Anti-bribery and anti-corruption laws
- Data privacy and protection regulations
- Competition and antitrust laws
- Export control and trade compliance rules

If you are unsure whether an action is legal or compliant, ask Legal or Compliance before proceeding.

## Reporting Concerns

### How to Report

You are encouraged to speak up about potential violations of this Code or other policies. You can report concerns to:
- Your manager
- Your HR Business Partner
- The Legal team
- The Ethics Hotline (anonymous option available)

Contact details are listed in the Employee Handbook and on the HR Portal.

### Non-Retaliation

Acme strictly prohibits retaliation against anyone who:
- Makes a good-faith report of suspected misconduct
- Participates in an investigation

Retaliation itself is treated as a serious violation and may result in discipline.

## Disciplinary Action

Violations of this Code or related policies may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment. The severity of action depends on factors such as:
- Nature and seriousness of the violation
- Whether it was intentional or repeated
- Impact on customers, colleagues, or the company

## Acknowledgment

All employees must acknowledge that they have read and understood this Code of Conduct:
- At the time of hire
- Annually during policy refresh

Acknowledgment is completed electronically in the HR Portal.

If you have questions about any part of this Code, contact HR or Legal for guidance before acting.
